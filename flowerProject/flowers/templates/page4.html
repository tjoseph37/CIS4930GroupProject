Grape Hyacinth

Symbolism: Sincery

Flower Color: Blue,yellow, white, purple.

Gardening Tips: Plant the bulbs in groups of ten or more, and be sure to set the bulbs twice as deep as they’re tall, and at least a couple of inches apart. Leaves will show up quickly. Just ignore them. Grape hyacinths send their leaves up out of the ground in the fall. This is strange because it’s just before winter comes and you would think they would not survive. Amazingly, they are very reliable every fall after the first year they grow. If you are wondering, “Do I prune grape hyacinths?” the answer is that you don’t have to. The plant will be fine if you do not. But if you want to neaten them up a little, a trim will not hurt the plant either. The grape hyacinth flower spikes don’t come until mid-spring. There may be some variation in color, depending on which ones you plant, but smoky blue is the most common color.Grape hyacinths don’t need a whole lot of care after they flower. They do fine with natural rainfall and don’t need fertilizer. Once their leaves die off, you can cut them back. In the fall, new leaves will grow, which will remind you of the pretty grape hyacinth flower to look forward to come spring once more.

Requires: Full Sunlight, cool soil

Best Season for Growth: Early spring, Late winter

Forget Me Not

Symbolism: True love, friendship

Flower Color:Blue

Gardening Tips: Forget-Me-Nuts are easy to grow. They will bloom profusely in shady areas, and do not require a lot of attention. They prefer shade, but will do well in sun, too. They grow well in average soils but the soil should be kept moist.Add a general purpose fertilizer once or twice a season.They may need mulching for winter protection in northern areas, or grow them as a biennial. Sow seeds after all danger of frost. Cover lightly with 1/8" of garden soil. Space them 4-5 inches apart and thin seedlings to 10" apart.

Requires: Full Sunlight, water an inch into the soil once a week

Best Season for Growth:Spring

Cornflower

Symbolism: Wealth, prosperity, fortune, friendship.

Flower Color: Various shades of blue and purple.

Gardening Tips:Young cornflower seedlings can tolerate freezing temperatures, so seeds can be planted in early spring, or in the fall where winters are mild. Sow cornflowers from early to mid spring for early summer bloom. Also sow in fall in climates with mild winters. Plant seeds in soil or in containers about 2 inches (5 cm) apart and one-half inch (1 cm) deep. Thin to 8 inches (20 cm) apart in all directions.Fast growth is encouraged by mixing a balanced organic fertilizer into the soil before planting

Requires: Full Sunlight, Don't overexpose to heat or cold.

Best Season for Growth:Fall, Spring/p>
